FAQ'S

                                                     

1.What exactly is an Oral & Maxillofacial Surgeon?

Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons (OMS) are specialists who treat conditions, injuries, and defects of the mouth, teeth, jaws, and face. Oral surgeons must complete
an additional 4 to 6 years of comprehensive training program after they graduate from dental school. Many oral surgeons attend programs that enable them to attend
medical school and obtain their medical degrees as well. Doctors Metz, Seidel and Orfanos have all completed their medical doctorates (MD)

3.How do I determine if I am a candidate for dental implants?

Almost every person who is missing one or more teeth and is in good general health is a candidate for dental implant treatment. Working together with your primary care
physician, we can optimize treatment for patients with diseases such as diabetes so that they are candidates for dental implants. The quality and quantity of available bone
is an important factor in dental implant treatment. Oral Maxillofacial surgeons can, even with significant bone loss, perform extraordinary procedures that add bone or create
new bone allow people who would not otherwise have been candidates for dental implants to have successful implant treatment.
